How we started.

With a total population of around 1.38 billion people, India is home to 1.8 million homeless people. However, what may come as a shock to you is that it is also home to 80 million stray cats and dogs, and that doesn’t even begin to cover all the animals in urgent need of help and protection. If that statistic doesn’t seem concerning enough, India was one of the countries hit hardest by the Covid-19 pandemic and to this date, India has suffered a total of 43 million cases. Considering that, the thought of 80 million innocent animals in just cats and dogs being forced to survive with zero love or empathy feels horrifying. While we may have the support of families and social circles, they don’t have access to these luxuries. They spend most of their lives starving on the streets and being subjected to trauma and injuries on a regular basis. They lose their innocence and grow distrusting, refusing help even when it’s offered. This reality is ignored more often than not and would need a coordinated approach from a passionate team along with the help of like-minded supporters. This is the problem we are attempting to tackle through this project.

Love

Majority of the time we find ourselves being compassionate towards an animal, that animal is domesticated, or a pet.People's love towards an animal should transcend such silly boundaries, only then can we truly understand the needs of stray animals and the pain they must feel.

Compassion

By showing empathy and putting forth a helping hand to strays in need. They might not say it but we understand. and it is this silence in our acknowledgement that can be the difference between thinking of saving a life, and actually doing it.

Endless Pawsabilities

With our team of hardworking and dedicated paw-patrols, we see endless pawsibilities, in a future where the streets of urban and rural areas are not classified by the "filth" of stray animals, but by the humanity that goes into helping them.
